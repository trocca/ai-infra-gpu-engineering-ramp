# Exam Booking Checklist

All three exams are booked through the NVIDIA certification portal and delivered as
**online proctored** exams (PSI/OnVUE-style). Details verified July 2026 — re-check each
exam's official page before paying.

## Where to book

1. Go to https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/learn/certification/ and open the specific exam page:
   - NCA-AIIO: …/ai-infrastructure-operations-associate/
   - NCP-GENL: …/generative-ai-llm-professional/
   - NCP-AIO: …/ai-operations-professional/
2. Follow the "Schedule Exam" link — it routes to NVIDIA's exam delivery partner. Create
   or reuse your candidate account (use the same email as your NVIDIA developer
   account so certs land in one profile).
3. Pick the date matching the plan: **end of week 4 / 8 / 12**. Book at the *start* of
   each month — a paid, dated exam is the best procrastination killer. Note the
   reschedule policy at checkout (typically free until 24–72 h before the slot).

## Online proctoring requirements (do this the week before, not exam morning)

- [ ] Government-issued photo ID, name matching your candidate account exactly.
- [ ] Run the proctor's system test on the actual machine + network you'll use
      (webcam, mic, screen share, bandwidth).
- [ ] One monitor only; external monitors unplugged. Close all apps; no virtual machines.
- [ ] Room: alone, door closed, desk cleared — no papers, second devices, headphones.
      You'll pan the webcam around the room at check-in.
- [ ] Stable connection — prefer wired Ethernet; disable OS updates/notifications.
- [ ] Check in ~30 min early. For NCP-AIO the hands-on labs run in a browser-based
      environment during the same session — the system test matters double there.

## After each exam

- [ ] Record result + date in `PROGRESS.md`.
- [ ] Claim the digital badge (Credly email) and add it to LinkedIn — for an evangelist /
      pre-sales role, the badge visibility is half the value.
- [ ] Note the expiry (2 years) in your calendar with a 3-month-before reminder.
- [ ] Same day, while fresh: write down which domains felt underweighted/overweighted in
      your prep — feeds the next month's plan.
